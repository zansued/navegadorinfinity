Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SharePass.

Is my password safe?
Absolutely. SharePass never sees, stores, or transmits your password. It shares encrypted session cookies, which are temporary access tokens that expire automatically. Your password stays on your device at all times.
What is a SPID?
SPID stands for SharePass ID. It's your unique username on SharePass, like a handle that your friends use to find you and share accounts with you. You choose it when you sign up.
Which websites work with SharePass?
SharePass works with virtually any website that uses cookie-based sessions, including Netflix, Disney+, Spotify, Amazon Prime, Hulu, and thousands more. If you can log in with a browser, SharePass can share it.
How long does shared access last?
You choose the duration when sharing: 4 hours, 1 day, 7 days, or 2 weeks. After the time expires, access is automatically revoked. You can also revoke access manually at any time.
Can the person I share with change my password?
No. SharePass shares a session cookie, not your credentials. The recipient is logged in as you but cannot see or change your password, email, or account settings on most platforms.
Is SharePass free?
Yes! SharePass is completely free to use. SharePass+ (our premium tier) offers additional features like longer sharing durations and priority support.
I already had an account but lost access. What do I do?
Use the "Recover your account" link in the sign-up form above. Enter the email you used when you created your SPID, and we'll send you a recovery code to restore your account.
